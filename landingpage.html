Let's Work Together to Change the World!

Our Mission Statement: To help reduce and educate individuals on the pollution in our oceans, while also working towards achieving ocean sustainability.

Why Should You Do This?

The ocean provides us with rain, drinking water, weather, food, climate, beaches, oxygen, and so much more. It is our duty, as humans, to protect it. Our non-profit organization, Life Below Water, provides individuals of all ages with the chance to give back to the Earth. We offer volunteer experiences in the community around the beaches near Jersey City and workshops given by passionate people on the topic of ocean pollution.
By 2050, there will be more plastic than fish in our oceans.

The amount of plastic in the ocean currently can circle the Earth more than 400 times.

When eating seafood, you are probably also eating plastic.

Less than 5% of plastic is recycled around the world.

What Can You Do to Help?
Become a Member
of "Life Below Water!"
To Become a Member of "Life Below Water," please fill out the form here. This will give you access to exclusive emails providing you with all of the opportunities on how to help volunteer in the community and participate in events through our organization to help protect our oceans. This is open to people of all ages. As you continue to volunteer, we will keep track of your hours, and make sure you are given credit through a certificate.

Donate!
If you would like to donate to help fund our organization, please fill out the form here. This will be very much appreciated by "Life Below Water," as the funds will help us continue providing with opportunities for future events and for our members.

Educate!
If you would like to volunteer by educating members of "Life Below Water" through informational workshops, please fill out the form here. These workshops will be done through Zoom or Microsoft Teams calls, where you would be expected to give a presentation.

Requirements:
  • Must be over 16 years of age
  • Must have some experience in the area of ocean pollution (i.e. other organizations, research, career in marine science, etc.)
You will be given credit for the number of community service hours you have done, and will receive a certificate for that. Once done with completing the form, we will get back to you on if you have been selected.
